LivestockGuard
Cow Zoonotic Disease Detection & Human Risk Assessment
Team: 3–4 members Duration: 1 month Web App + ML One Health India-focused

LivestockGuard is a multimodal zoonotic disease intelligence system that bridges animal disease detection with human risk assessment — a gap no existing Indian app addresses. It detects cow diseases visually and through symptoms, then uniquely calculates the farmer's personal infection risk and triggers community-level outbreak alerts.

Core novel contribution: Most livestock apps stop at "your cow is sick." LivestockGuard says "your cow is sick AND here is your risk of catching it, and here is what to watch for over the next 30 days."
What makes it different
🔬
Multimodal detection
CV model for visible diseases (LSD, FMD, Ringworm) + symptom ML for invisible ones (Brucellosis, Leptospirosis, bTB). Mirrors real vet practice.
⚠️
Human risk scoring
After animal disease is flagged, a Bayesian model calculates the farmer's personal infection probability based on contact history and exposure type.
📍
Cluster detection
GPS-tagged reports feed a DBSCAN algorithm. 3+ cases within 10 km in 7 days triggers an outbreak cluster alert across the region.
📅
Watch calendar
Generates a personalized human symptom watch calendar based on the disease's incubation period. Tells farmer exactly what to watch for and when.
Cow zoonotic diseases covered
  • Visually detectable (CV model): Lumpy Skin Disease, Foot & Mouth Disease, Bovine Ringworm
  • Symptom-based (ML classifier): Brucellosis, Leptospirosis, Bovine Tuberculosis, Anthrax, Q Fever
  • Human risk layer covers all 8 diseases with disease-specific transmission pathways, incubation periods, and exposure risk calculations

Every feature is designed around the zoonotic angle — not just animal health, but the human risk that follows from it.

📸
F1 — Animal photo disease classifier
CV model
Farmer uploads or takes a photo of their cow. EfficientNet-B0 (fine-tuned) classifies it as Healthy, LSD, FMD, or Ringworm with a confidence score. Grad-CAM heatmap highlights which part of the image triggered the prediction.
  • Accepts camera capture or gallery upload
  • Works on low-res mobile photos
  • Returns confidence score + visual explanation
  • If confidence <60%, prompts farmer to proceed to symptom checker
📋
F2 — Symptom-based disease screener
ML classifier
A structured checklist covering 18 symptoms mapped to 8 cow zoonotic diseases. Random Forest / XGBoost classifier outputs the most likely disease with probability. Handles invisible diseases that CV cannot detect (Brucellosis, Leptospirosis, bTB, Anthrax).
  • Symptoms: abortion, milk drop, jaundice, fever, coughing, weight loss, sudden death, nasal discharge, swollen lymph nodes, limping, blisters, skin lesions, diarrhea, eye discharge, difficulty breathing, lethargy, blood from orifices, reproductive failure
  • Outputs top-3 probable diseases with confidence percentages
  • Fusion layer combines CV + symptom scores when both are available
🧬
F3 — Bayesian human exposure risk scorer
Novel
After disease is identified, farmer answers a short 6-question exposure form. A Bayesian network computes a Human Infection Risk Score (0–100) and risk tier.
  • Questions: direct contact without gloves? consumed raw milk/meat? open wounds? children under 10 in contact? elderly/pregnant family members? vaccination status?
  • Output: risk score, tier (Low/Moderate/High/Critical), and primary transmission route explanation
  • Each disease has disease-specific Bayesian prior probabilities from WHO literature
  • Score updates dynamically as farmer answers each question
🔗
F4 — Zoonotic transmission pathway visualizer
Novel
Interactive diagram showing exactly how the detected disease travels from cow to human. Built as an interactive SVG/React component.
  • Shows: animal reservoir → transmission route (direct/airborne/milk/meat/urine/vectors) → human entry point → target organs
  • Color-coded risk nodes (red = high risk pathway)
  • Shows which household members are at highest risk
  • Tap any node to learn more about that step
📅
F5 — Incubation-based human watch calendar
Novel
Generates a personalized calendar from the exposure date to end of incubation period, with daily symptom reminders.
  • Example (Brucellosis): "Exposure Oct 3 → Watch for fever, joint pain, night sweats between Oct 8–Nov 2"
  • Calendar shows start date, danger window, symptoms to watch, and end of risk period
  • Push notification reminders (PWA) during the watch window
  • One-tap "I have symptoms" button that opens a pre-filled doctor visit report
🗺️
F6 — Geospatial cluster detection
Novel
Every report is anonymously GPS-tagged. DBSCAN algorithm runs on disease-location-time data. Farmers see a live heatmap of disease activity near them.
  • Cluster trigger: 3+ cases, same disease, within 10 km, within 7 days
  • Alert pushed to all users in cluster zone
  • Interactive Leaflet.js heatmap with disease filters
  • Cluster severity indicator (moderate / high / critical)
  • Authorities notified automatically on critical cluster formation
📤
F7 — One-tap authority reporting
Supporting
For notifiable zoonotic diseases (Rabies, Brucellosis, Anthrax, FMD, bTB), auto-generates a pre-filled official disease report PDF.
  • Pre-filled with: GPS coordinates, disease name, confidence score, animal count, human exposure count, date
  • One-tap submission to nearest DAHD/veterinary officer
  • Farmer receives a reference number for follow-up
  • Report stored locally for farmer's records
🏥
F8 — Nearest veterinary center locator
Supporting
Uses GPS + OpenStreetMap to show nearest government veterinary centers, private vets, and PHCs (for human cases) on an interactive map.
  • Distance and estimated travel time
  • Tap to call / get directions
  • Filters for: govt vet / private vet / human PHC

End-to-end data flow from farmer input to actionable output. Each layer adds a new dimension of intelligence.

1
Input layer
Farmer uploads cow photo (camera/gallery) + fills symptom checklist + answers exposure history form. GPS auto-detected. All inputs optional — system degrades gracefully with partial data.
2
Detection layer — CV model
EfficientNet-B0 (fine-tuned on combined cow disease dataset) classifies the image → disease label + confidence score. Grad-CAM generates visual explanation heatmap. If confidence <60%, model flags uncertainty.
3
Detection layer — symptom ML
Random Forest / XGBoost classifier takes 18-symptom binary vector → top-3 disease probabilities. Covers invisible diseases (Brucellosis, Leptospirosis, Anthrax, bTB, Q Fever) that CV cannot detect.
4
Fusion layer
Weighted ensemble combines CV score + symptom score (weights tuned on validation set). If only one input available, uses that alone. Outputs single final disease prediction with confidence.
5
Zoonotic intelligence layer (core novel contribution)
Runs 4 parallel processes: (a) Transmission pathway engine looks up disease-specific routes from DB. (b) Bayesian risk scorer calculates human infection probability. (c) DBSCAN cluster detector checks if this case forms a cluster. (d) Watch calendar generator computes date range from incubation period.
6
Storage layer
Report saved to PostgreSQL + PostGIS. GPS coordinates indexed for fast geospatial queries. User's watch calendar stored locally (PWA). Report PDF generated for authority submission.
7
Output dashboard
Farmer sees: disease name + confidence, human risk score (0–100) + tier, transmission pathway diagram, watch calendar, regional cluster map, nearest vet/PHC, one-tap authority report button.

Each module is independently developed by one team member and integrated in Week 3.

🖼️
Module 1 — Computer Vision Disease Classifier
Model: EfficientNet-B0, pretrained on ImageNet, fine-tuned on cow disease dataset
Classes: Healthy, Lumpy Skin Disease, Foot & Mouth Disease, Bovine Ringworm
Training data: ~3,700 images → augmented to ~7,500 (Albumentations: flips, brightness, contrast, mosaic)
Explainability: Grad-CAM heatmap overlaid on original image
Threshold: Confidence <60% triggers "uncertain — please fill symptoms" prompt
Why only these 3 diseases? Only visually diagnosable cow zoonotic diseases with available datasets. High-risk invisible diseases handled by Module 2.
📊
Module 2 — Symptom-Based ML Classifier
Model: Random Forest (primary) + XGBoost (ensemble)
Input: 18-dimensional binary symptom vector
Diseases covered: LSD, FMD, Ringworm, Brucellosis, Leptospirosis, Bovine TB, Anthrax, Q Fever (8 classes)
Training data: Symptom-disease matrix built from WHO/FAO/CFSPH disease fact sheets. ~500–800 synthetic patient records generated using the symptom matrix probabilities.
Output: Top-3 diseases with probability percentages
Fusion: When CV also available, weighted average: 0.6×CV + 0.4×Symptom (weights tuned on val set)
🧮
Module 3 — Bayesian Human Risk Scorer
Library: pgmpy (Python Bayesian Networks)
Structure: Disease → Transmission Route → Exposure Type → Risk Score
Evidence nodes (farmer inputs): direct contact, raw milk/meat consumed, open wounds, children in contact, elderly/pregnant family, vaccination status
Prior probabilities: sourced from WHO transmission probability literature per disease
Output: Human infection probability (0–1) → displayed as score (0–100) and tier: Low (<20), Moderate (20–50), High (50–80), Critical (>80)
Per-disease examples: Brucellosis via raw milk contact → base P=0.15; with open wounds → P=0.40; with unvaccinated child → P=0.55
📍
Module 4 — Geospatial Cluster Detector
Algorithm: DBSCAN (Density-Based Spatial Clustering)
Parameters: epsilon=10km, min_samples=3, time_window=7 days
Input: All GPS-tagged disease reports from PostGIS database
Output: Cluster label per report, cluster centroid, cluster severity (based on disease type + case count)
Alert logic: New report triggers DBSCAN re-run on nearby points. If cluster formed → push notification to all users within 15km radius
Visualization: Leaflet.js choropleth heatmap with cluster boundaries
📅
Module 5 — Watch Calendar Generator
Disease incubation database: JSON lookup table for all 8 diseases (min/max incubation in humans, sourced from WHO)
Input: Disease name + date of exposure
Output: Start date, danger window (min–max incubation), symptoms to watch, end date
Examples: FMD: 2–6 days | Brucellosis: 5–60 days | Anthrax: 1–5 days | bTB: months–years (flags for regular screening)
PWA notifications: Daily push reminders during the watch window
UI: Timeline bar showing today, window start, window end, with symptom cards
🌐
Module 6 — PWA Frontend
Framework: React + Tailwind CSS, configured as Progressive Web App
Key screens: Home (quick report), Photo upload, Symptom checklist, Risk dashboard, Cluster map, Watch calendar, Vet locator, Report history
Offline support: Service worker caches last report and vet locations
Accessibility: Large touch targets (rural mobile users), minimal text on critical screens (icon-first), multilingual (English + Hindi placeholder)
Performance: Lazy-loaded routes, compressed model inference via API (no on-device ML)

All datasets are free and open access. Strategy: combine all image sources, augment heavily, use symptom matrix for invisible diseases.

Key insight: High-risk diseases (Brucellosis, Leptospirosis, bTB, Anthrax) have NO viable image datasets because they show no external visual symptoms. These are handled by the symptom ML module — which is actually more accurate for these diseases.

Final combined CV dataset
  • LSD: ~1,500 images (3 Kaggle sources combined)
  • FMD: ~400 images (Kaggle + Roboflow combined)
  • Ringworm: ~400 images (SLIIT + CFSPH)
  • Healthy: ~800 images (extracted from LSD datasets)
  • Total before augmentation: ~3,100 images
  • Total after augmentation (Albumentations): ~7,500–8,000 images
  • Augmentations: horizontal flip, brightness/contrast jitter, rotation ±15°, Gaussian noise, mosaic, cutout

API endpoints (FastAPI backend)
  • POST /detect/image — accepts image file, returns disease + confidence + Grad-CAM heatmap URL
  • POST /detect/symptoms — accepts symptom vector, returns top-3 disease probabilities
  • POST /risk/human — accepts disease + exposure form, returns Bayesian risk score
  • GET /clusters/nearby — accepts lat/lng, returns nearby active disease clusters
  • POST /report/submit — saves GPS-tagged report to DB, triggers cluster check
  • GET /vets/nearby — accepts lat/lng, returns nearest vet centers
  • POST /authority/report — generates and submits authority PDF report
